# Translation Gaps

This document tracks AI safety and AI-related terms that have no direct, natural equivalent in Hausa — where the concept must be explained rather than simply translated, or where no equivalent currently exists at all.

Each entry records: the English term, its meaning, the current Hausa status, and (where one exists) the approximation currently in use. This is a living document — contributions and corrections from Hausa speakers are welcome (see `/community` for how to contribute).

**Status key:**
- 🔴 **No equivalent** — no Hausa word or established phrase exists; the concept must be explained from scratch
- 🟡 **Approximated** — a working phrase exists but requires explanation and doesn't fully capture the term's meaning
- 🟢 **Equivalent exists** — a Hausa term is in use and reasonably captures the meaning

---

## 🔴 Prompt (AI/technical sense)

**English meaning:** The instruction or input a user gives to an AI system to generate a response.

**Hausa status:** No equivalent — not approximate, not borrowed. Nothing currently in use.

**Why this matters:** Without a term for "prompt," it becomes difficult to explain prompt injection, prompt engineering, or any concept that assumes a shared understanding of what a "prompt" is. This is foundational vocabulary that safety evaluation and public education both depend on.

---

## 🔴 Alignment / Misalignment

**English meaning:** Whether an AI system's behaviour matches the intentions and values of the people who built or are using it.

**Hausa status:** No equivalent.

**Why this matters:** Alignment is one of the core organizing concepts of AI safety. Without a Hausa term, any discussion of "is this AI system doing what we actually want" has to be rebuilt from first principles each time, rather than referencing a shared concept.

---

## 🔴 Autonomous agent

**English meaning:** An AI system that can take actions or make decisions on its own, without a human directly prompting each step.

**Hausa status:** No equivalent.

**Why this matters:** As AI systems move from simple chatbots to systems that act on users' behalf (booking things, making decisions, taking multi-step actions), the ability to explain *what kind* of system someone is dealing with becomes a basic safety and consent issue.

---

## 🔴 Existential risk

**English meaning:** A risk serious enough to permanently and drastically curtail humanity's future potential — used in AI safety to describe worst-case, civilization-scale harms.

**Hausa status:** No equivalent.

**Why this matters:** Community risk communication (the kind used in vaccine education, disaster preparedness, etc.) has no equivalent register for this category of risk when it comes to AI. This affects how AI risk can be communicated to policymakers and communities alike.

---

## 🟡 Deepfakes

**English meaning:** Fake but realistic images, audio, or video generated or altered by AI to make it appear someone said or did something they didn't.

**Hausa status:** Approximated — no single word exists, but the concept can be explained with a full sentence, e.g. describing it as *"an image, sound, or video that is not real, but made by a computer to look and sound like it is real."*

**Why this matters:** Because there's no single term, every explanation requires a full descriptive sentence — this makes the concept harder to reference quickly in warnings, public health messaging, or community discussions where brevity matters (e.g., radio announcements, SMS alerts).
