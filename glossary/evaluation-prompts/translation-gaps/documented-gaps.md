# Documented Translation Gaps

This file records AI safety concepts that have no direct Hausa equivalent,
based on fieldwork conducted during the Safer Schools, Safer Communities 
community project in Gombe State, northern Nigeria.

**Documented by:** Hadiya Usman | June 2026

---

## Deepfakes

**English definition:** A synthetic image, video, or audio recording that has 
been manipulated using AI to make it appear that a real person said or did 
something they did not.

**Hausa equivalent:** None.

**Approximation used:** An image or recording that has been convincingly altered 
and manipulated to misrepresent someone as doing or saying something that was 
not actually done or said (full explanatory sentence required).

**Cultural note:** The concept of deliberate visual deception exists in Hausa 
culture, but the specific mechanism of AI-generated synthetic media has no 
cultural anchor or vocabulary. The word "deepfake" borrowed directly from 
English carries no meaning to a Hausa speaker unfamiliar with AI.

---

## Prompt (AI meaning)

**English definition:** The instruction or input that a human gives to an AI 
system to generate a response or take an action.

**Hausa equivalent:** None. Not an approximate one. Not a borrowed one.

**Approximation used:** [Currently unresolved — contributions welcome]

**Cultural note:** The everyday Hausa meaning of the closest approximation 
relates to direct verbal instruction in a human context, which does not 
capture the asynchronous, text-based, machine-processed nature of an AI prompt.

---

## Alignment (AI safety meaning)

**English definition:** The degree to which an AI system's goals and behaviours 
match the intentions and values of its designers and users.

**Hausa equivalent:** None.

**Approximation used:** [Currently unresolved — contributions welcome]

**Cultural note:** The concept of alignment between human intention and machine 
behaviour has no established Hausa framework. The closest available concepts 
relate to human-to-human compliance or obedience, which carry different 
connotations.
